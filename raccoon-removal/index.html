Recognizing the Signs of a Raccoon Problem

Knowing the signs that indicate a raccoon presence on your property in Apple Valley, MN is crucial for addressing the issue quickly and effectively. Ignoring these signs can lead to more significant problems, including structural damage to your home and potential health hazards. Raccoons are often more active during the night, so some signs might be more obvious during those hours.

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common indicators of a raccoon infestation is hearing strange noises coming from your attic, walls, or crawl spaces, particularly at night. These noises can include scratching, thumping, vocalizations like growls or chirps, and movement sounds. Raccoons are relatively large animals, and their movements can be quite noticeable. They may be nesting, searching for food, or simply moving around their harborage area. These sounds can be quite unsettling and disruptive to your sleep.

If you're hearing persistent noises, especially during the evening or early morning hours, it's a strong possibility that you have a raccoon or another wildlife intruder. Don't ignore these sounds; they are the animal telling you they are there. The longer you wait, the more damage or contamination they might cause. Professional inspection can confirm the source of the noises and determine the best course of action for removal.

Damaged Garbage Cans and Scattered Trash

Raccoons are notorious for raiding garbage cans in search of food. If you find your trash cans overturned, lids removed, and garbage scattered around your property, raccoons are likely the culprits. Their strong paws and dexterity allow them to easily open unsecured bins. This messy behavior is not only unsightly but also attracts other pests and can create unsanitary conditions.

Securing your garbage cans is a key step in deterring raccoons. However, if they have already established a habit of visiting your property for food, simply securing the trash might not be enough to make them leave. They may continue to explore other areas of your property, looking for alternative food sources or shelter. Consistent garbage damage is a clear signal that you need to address the underlying issue of raccoons on your property.

Presence of Droppings

Raccoon droppings, also known as scat, are another tell-tale sign of their presence. Raccoon droppings are usually tubular and can vary in size and content depending on their diet. They often deposit their scat in communal areas called latrines, which can be located in attics, decks, patios, or along fences. These latrines can become quite large over time and pose significant health risks.

It is extremely important to avoid direct contact with raccoon droppings, as they can contain roundworm eggs and other harmful pathogens. If you discover what you suspect are raccoon droppings on your property, it's best to leave the cleanup to professionals who have the necessary protective equipment and knowledge to handle it safely. Identifying and cleaning up these latrines is a critical part of the raccoon removal process to protect the health of your family and pets.

Damage to Structures

Raccoons can cause significant damage to your home as they attempt to gain entry or create nesting sites. This damage can include torn screens, damaged vents, lifted shingles, broken soffits, and enlarged openings in foundations or walls. They are surprisingly strong and persistent when they are trying to access a sheltered area, such as an attic or crawl space, to build a den.

Inspecting the exterior of your home regularly for signs of damage is important for identifying potential entry points before they become a major problem. Areas around the roofline, chimneys, and porches are common places where raccoons might try to gain access. Addressing these vulnerabilities promptly can prevent a raccoon infestation and the costly repairs that can result from their activity. Don't underestimate the ability of a raccoon to exploit weaknesses in your home's structure.

Evidence of Nesting Material

Raccoons seek out safe and sheltered places to build their dens, especially during breeding season. They may use materials they find, such as insulation, leaves, and debris, to create a comfortable nesting site. Finding evidence of nesting material in your attic, crawl space, or under a deck can indicate that a raccoon is denning on your property. These dens are often where female raccoons will raise their young.

Locating a raccoon den requires careful inspection and understanding of their habits. Disturbing a den with young raccoons can be dangerous, as the mother will be very protective. It is always best to have a professional assess the situation and determine the safest way to remove the animals, especially if there are kits involved. Identifying and removing nesting material is also important for preventing future infestations in the same location.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets, particularly dogs, may alert you to the presence of raccoons on your property. Dogs may become agitated, bark excessively towards certain areas of the house or yard, or try to chase after something you can't see. Their keen sense of smell and hearing can detect the presence of wildlife, including raccoons, long before you do.

Paying attention to your pet's behavior can provide early warning signs of a raccoon problem. If your dog is unusually focused on a particular area, especially at night, it's worth investigating further. Keeping pets safe from potential encounters with raccoons is also important, as raccoons can be aggressive and carry diseases like rabies. Securing pet food and water outdoors can also help prevent attracting raccoons to your yard.
